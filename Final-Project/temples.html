Temples

While we hope to someday service Temple patrons at every Temple location, we currently only service four areas. Please see the visit the pages below for more details on each Temple.

Salt Lake, Utah Temple

Plans to construct the temple were announced July 28, 1847. Brigham Young, president of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints from 1847 to 1877, broke ground February 14, 1853, and Wilford Woodruff, Church president from 1889 to 1898, dedicated the Salt Lake Temple April 6, 1893. The temple serves Latter-day Saints in Salt Lake City, Tooele and eastern Nevada.

Jordan River, Utah Temple

Plans to construct the temple were announced 3 February 1978, Church leaders broke ground 9 June 1979, and President Marion G. Romney, a counselor in the First Presidency of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints, dedicated the Jordan River Utah Temple 16 November 1981. The temple serves Latter-day Saints in the southern valley of Salt Lake County.

Seattle, Washington Temple

Plans to construct the temple were announced 15 November 1975, Church leaders broke ground 27 May 1978, and Spencer W. Kimball, president of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints from 1973 to 1985, dedicated the Seattle Washington Temple 17 November 1980. The temple serves Latter-day Saints in western Washington.

Rexburg, Idaho Temple

Plans to construct the temple were announced 12 December 2003, Church leaders broke ground 30 July 2005, and Thomas S. Monson, president of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints, dedicated the Rexburg Idaho Temple 10 February 2008. The temple serves Latter-day Saints in Rexburg and eastern Idaho.
